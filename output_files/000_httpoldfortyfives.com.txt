    <td rowspan="2"><p align="justify" class="style1"><span class="style7">W</span>elcome to Old Forty Fives.com, where the '50s and '60s come back to life.</p>
        <p align="justify" class="style1"><span class="style7">W</span>hen did Rock'n Roll start?</p>
        <p align="justify" class="style1"> <span class="style7">S</span>ome people think it started in 1953 with Ike Turner's &quot;My Rocket 88,&quot; or Big Joe Turner with &quot;Honey, Hush&quot; in 1953, or &quot;Shake, Rattle and Roll&quot;  1954. Personally, I believe it started with the premiere of the movie Blackboard Jungle  in theaters across the country, in 1955. This movie featured the song &quot;Rock Around the Clock&quot; by &quot;Bill Haley and his Comets,&quot; which helped to propel Rock and Roll as a musical genre. It was reported that teenagers jumped from their seats to dance to the song.</p>
        <p align="justify" class="style1"><span class="style10">I</span>t is Alan Freed, in 1952-53 who is credited with coining the  phrase &quot;Rock'n Roll.&quot; He visited a Cleveland record store and learned that  R&amp;B records were being snapped up by white teenagers, and immediately sensing the  makings of something big, he changed the name of his popular music show on  radio station WJW from  &quot;Record Rendezvous&quot; to &quot;Moon  Dog's Rock 'n' Roll House Party&quot; and began playing R&amp;B tunes. Freed  apparently used the term &quot;Rock 'n' Roll&quot; to describe the music  because he thought the racial connotation of &quot;rhythm and blues&quot; might  turn off the white audience. </p>
        <p align="justify" class="style1"> <span class="style7">I</span> remember, I was about 11-12 years old at the time and just starting to get into  music,  and this new music genre did not sit well with my old man at the time. Songs, like &quot;Lollipop&quot; by the Chordettes, would bring my dad running across the house, or yard, just to shut off the radio, or change the station to a Country &amp; Western music station.</p>
